About Me

I am an information professional with interest in information democracy, knowledge management and indigenous knowledge systems and its applications in solving contemporary challenges affecting the global communities. Currently working, reading and researching in the field of alcohol, and drug abuse.

I am an advocate for Information Democracy seeking to achieve a free and informed society by promoting access and encourage participation of people and communities through providing necessary ethical and structural framework(s) for equitable access to both scientific and Indigenous knowledge on top ensuring that IKS holders are not marginalized. I also through Knowledge Management (KM), empower communities by providing context-specific solutions that are highly sustainable and culturally relevant, and recommend tools and technologies, procedures and processes to support capture, storage, dissemination and integration of IKS and formal scientific knowledge in ways that are open accessible to all and available for use by all members of the community.
